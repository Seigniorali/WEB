John Lennon

John Winston Lennon (9 October 1940 - 8 December 1980) was a British rock musician, singer, poet, composer, artist, writer and activist. One of the founders and a member of The Beatles. He is one of the most popular musicians of the XX century. After the breakup of The Beatles, he began a solo career, but in 1980 he was killed by a former fan of the band. In addition to his musical activities, Lennon was also known as a political activist. He expressed his views both in songs and in public speeches. The famous song "Imagine" expresses Lennon's thoughts about how the world should be arranged. Lennon preached the ideas of equality and brotherhood of people, peace, freedom. This made him a hippie idol and one of the most significant public figures of the 1960s- 1970s.



Paul McCartney

Sir James Paul McCartney (18 June 1942) is a British musician, multi-instrumentalist, writer and producer. One of the founders of The Beatles, 16-time Grammy Award winner, Knight Bachelor and Knight of the Order of the British Empire. The Lennon-McCartney duo has become one of the most influential and successful copyright unions in the history of modern music. Paul McCartney has been repeatedly included in the Guinness Book of Records, in particular, as the most successful musician and composer of recent history: his 60 discs have a "golden" status, the total circulation of singles exceeded 100 million, and the song "Yesterday" holds the first place in the number of recorded cover versions of it



George Harrison

George Harrison (25 February 1943 - 29 November 2001) was a British rock musician, singer, songwriter, writer, producer, sitarist and guitarist, best known as the lead guitarist of The Beatles. Harrison was not only a singer, songwriter, guitarist and sitarist, but also a music producer and innovator. In the 1960s, Harrison converted to Hinduism, which significantly influenced his subsequent creative and social activities. Harrison played a key role in awakening interest in Indian culture, Hinduism and the Hare Krishna Movement in the West. Harrison also wrote and performed several songs on each of the band's albums. From the late period of the ensemble's work, Harrison's authorship includes such hits as "Here Comes the Sun", "Something" and "While My Guitar Gently Weeps". After the breakup of the band, Harrison began a successful solo career, releasing the first "triple" album in the history of music, All Things Must Pass, which became the first solo album by one of the ex-Beatles to occupy the top position in the charts. The song "My Sweet Lord" was released as a separate single, which also reached the top of the charts. Harrison died on November 29, 2001 in Los Angeles from lung cancer and brain cancer.



Ringo Star

Sir Ringo Starr (7 July 1940) is a British musician, songwriter, and actor. He is known as the drummer of The Beatles. Knight Bachelor of the Order of the British Empire. On August 18, 1962, Ringo officially joined the Beatles Quartet. Ringo played drums and percussion on all the Beatles songs. Although Ringo's vocals on Beatles recordings can be heard infrequently, Ringo played leading roles in Beatles films. It is believed that Ringo Starr of all four "Beatles" had the greatest acting talent. After the breakup of the Beatles, Ringo Starr starred in several more films.
