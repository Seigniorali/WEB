General Infomation

The Beatles (also called the "Liverpool Four") are a British rock band from Liverpool, founded in 1960, which included John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison and Ringo Starr. Stuart Sutcliffe, Pete Best and Jimmy Nichol also performed in the band at various times. Most of The Beatles' compositions are co-authored and signed with the names of John Lennon and Paul McCartney. The discography of the band includes 13 official studio albums published in 1963-1970, and 211 songs.



Songs and success

Starting with imitating the classics of American rock and roll of the 1950s, The Beatles came to their own style and sound. The Beatles had a significant impact on rock music and are recognized by experts as one of the most successful bands of the XX century, both creatively and commercially. Many famous rock musicians admit that they became such under the influence of The Beatles' songs. The quartet became the first British band whose records gained popularity and first places in the US charts, and with it began the worldwide recognition of British bands, as well as the "Liverpool" sound of rock music. The band's musicians and their producer and sound engineer George Martin own innovative developments in the field of sound recording, combining various styles, including symphonic and psychedelic music, as well as filming video clips.
